\begin{abstract}
We outline a proof of a version of the isoperimetric inequality in terms of Favard area.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Given a compact subset $K\subset \RR^n$ let us denote by $a(K)$ the Favard area; that is, the average of the areas (where area means $(n-1)$-dimensional Hausdorff measure) of orthogonal projections of $K$ to hyperplanes.
Further, let us denote by $\vol$ the $n$-dimensional Hausdorff measure.

\begin{thm}{Theorem}\label{thm:main}
Let $K\subset \RR^n$ be a compact subset and let $B\subset \RR^n$ be a ball.
Then
\[a(K)= a(B)\quad\Rightarrow\quad \vol K\le \vol B.\]
Moreover, if equality holds, then $K$ is congruent to $B$.
\end{thm}

By Cauchy's projection formula, we have $a(K)\le \tfrac{\omega_{n-1}}{n\cdot\omega_n}\cdot \area (\partial K)$.
Therefore our theorem implies the standard isoperimetric inequality.

In the proof we adapt the idea of Frederick Almgren, \cite[pp. 454--455]{almgren-1986}.

\section{Plan}

Given a compact set $K$, consider the ball $B$ such that $a(K)=a(B)$.
Let us denote by $\kappa=\kappa(K)$ the normal curvature of $\partial B$,
so $\tfrac1\kappa$ is the radius of $B$ and
\[\frac{\omega_{n-1}}{\kappa^{n-1}}=a(K)=a(B),\quad \frac{\omega_{n}}{\kappa^{n}}=\vol B.\]

\begin{thm}{Lemma}\label{lem:support}
Let $K$ be a compact set in $\RR^n$.
Given $\eps>0$, there is a smooth convex body $C$ with smooth boundary such that $C\supset K$,
$\partial C\cap K$ contains a single point, say $p$,
and the Gauss curvature of $\partial C$ at $p$ is at least $(\kappa(K)-\eps)^{n-1}$;
in particular, the mean curvature of $\partial C$ at $p$ is at least $(n-1)\cdot (\kappa(K)-\eps)$.
\end{thm}

\parit{Sketch.}
Let us denote by $K_s$ the closed $s$-neighborhood of $K$.
Note that it is sufficient to solve the problem for $K_s$ with sufficiently small $s>0$,
once it is done, an equidistant surface solves the original problem.

Good thing about $K_s$ is that the convex hull $\conv K_s$ has $C^{1,1}$-smooth boundary.
So shape operator $\Shape$ and the Gauss curvature $G=\det(\Shape)$ are defined almost everywhere on $\partial(\conv K_s)$.

Define the \emph{contact boundary} of $K_s$ as
\[\cont K_s=K_s\cap\partial(\conv K_s).\]
Note that the spherical image of $\cont K_s$ is the whole sphere.
It follows that
\[\int\limits_{x\in \cont K_s}G(x)=\area \SSS^{n-1}=n\cdot\omega_n.\]
Therefore,
\[G(x)\ge \frac{\area \SSS^{n-1}}{\area (\cont K_s)}-\eps/3\]
for $x$ in a set of positive measure of $\cont K_s$.

By Steiner's formula, $\area (\cont K_s)$ is close to $\area (\cont K)$ for small $s>0$.
Clearly,
\[a(\cont K_s)\le a(K_s).\]
Further, since $\cont K_s\subset \partial (\conv K_s)$ we have
\[\area (\cont K_s)\le \frac{\area \SSS^{n-1}}{\omega_{n-1}}\cdot a(\cont K_s);\]
the constant $\tfrac{\area \SSS^{n-1}}{\omega_{n-1}}$ here is the optimal constant (so we have equality if $\cont K_s$ is a closed convex hypersurface).

Combining the last three inequalities we get a point $x\in \cont K_s$ with well-defined shape operator and Gauss curvature $(\kappa(K)-\eps/2)^{n-1}$.
Let us take a smooth hypersurface $H$ with slightly smaller shape operator at $p$.
Let $H'$ be the equidistant hypersurface that touches $K$; we can assume that $H'$ is still convex and smooth at this point, say $p$.
It remains to take a small neighborhood of $\conv K$, chop it by $H'$ and smooth the resulting body without changing it near $p$ and keeping it convex.
\qeds


\begin{thm}{Lemma}\label{lem:variation}
Let $C$, $K$, and $p$ as in the previous lemma, and let
\[K_t=\set{x\in K}{\dist_{\partial C}x\ge t}.\]
Then
\[a(K)-a(K_t)\ge \frac{\omega_{n-1}}{n\omega_n}\cdot(H+o_t(1))\cdot (\vol K-\vol K_t),\]
where $H$ denotes the mean curvature of $\partial C$ at $p$.
\end{thm}

\parit{Sketch.}
Let $x$ be a point in a small neighborhood of $p$.
Denote by $\bar x\in\partial C$ the closest point to $x$,
let $\nu(x)$ be the outer unit normal at $\bar x$ to $C$ and $r(x)\z=|x-\bar x|$.
The pair $(\nu(x),r(x))$ completely describes $x$ and it gives a smooth parametrization of a neighborhood of $p$ by an open subset in $\SSS^{n-1}\times \RR$.
These local coordinates have jacobian $1/G(p)$ at $p$, and since everything is smooth,
its jacobian at $x$ is $1/G(p)+O(|x-p|)$.

It remains to write both sides using these coordinates (but it takes some effort).
\qeds

\parit{Proof of \ref{thm:main}.}
Assume $K$ is a counterexample;
that is, we can choose $\delta>0$ such that
\[s_\delta(K)\df\vol K- (1+\delta)\cdot \vol B>0,\]
where $B$ is the ball such that $a(K)= a(B)$.

Without loss of generality, we can assume that nonempty intersection of $K$ with any open set has positive volume.

Applying \ref{lem:variation} to $C$ and $p$ are provided by \ref{lem:support}, we get a compact set $K'\subset K$ such that
\[\vol K-\vol K'< \frac{\omega_{n-1}}{n\cdot\omega_n}\cdot((n-1)\cdot\kappa(K)-\eps)\cdot (a(K)-a(K')).\]
For appropriate choice of $\eps>0$, we get $s_\delta(K')>s_\delta(K)$.
Applying Zorn's lemma, we arrive at a contradiction.
\qeds
